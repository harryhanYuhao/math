\subsubsection{Case 1,0}

Let us calculate what does $\vec{Q}_1, \vec{Q}_2$ do with state $1, 0$.
Use our calculation in \eqref{q2-1-cal}, we see, if $v$ is the eigenvector of $\vec{P}$ with eigenvalue $1$, and $w$ is the eigenvector with eigenvalue $-1$, then 

\begin{equation}\label{q2-2-10-cal}
	\begin{split}
	v 
	&\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_1} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} |1\rangle \otimes v 
	\xrightarrow{\Pi^0 \vec{Q}_2} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|+\rangle \otimes v  \\
	w 
	&\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_1}  |0\rangle \otimes w 
	\xrightarrow{\Pi^0 \vec{Q}_2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|+\rangle \otimes w  \\
	\end{split}
\end{equation}

Using our notation in \eqref{q2-1}, we see the operation which is the left hand side of \eqref{question:q2} is 
$$
\sqrt{2} | 0 \rangle \otimes W 
$$

Our calculation in \eqref{q2-1-cal} shows that 
\begin{equation}
	\begin{split}
		v &\xrightarrow{\sqrt{2} | 0 \rangle \otimes W} \sqrt{2} |0\rangle \otimes v \\
		w &\xrightarrow{\sqrt{2} | 0 \rangle \otimes W} \sqrt{2} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} |0\rangle \otimes w
	\end{split}
\end{equation}

Comparing with \eqref{q2-2-10-cal}, we see we need to find a Clifford $C_{0,1}$ such that 
\begin{equation}
	\begin{split}
		C_{0, 1} |+\rangle \otimes v & = 2 e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} |0\rangle \otimes v \\
		C_{0, 1} |+\rangle \otimes w & = 2 e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} |0\rangle \otimes w \\
	\end{split}
\end{equation}

Let $P = N^{\dagger} Z_1 N$, where $Z_1$ is the Pauli $Z$ acting on the first qubit. 
I claim that $C_{0,1} = H \otimes N^{\dagger} S_1 N$ works, where $S_1 = e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} |0 \rangle \langle 0| +  e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} |1 \rangle \langle 1|$
The verification is straightforward, as $H |+\rangle = 2 |0\rangle$,
and $N^{\dagger}S_1N$ exactly sends $v$ to $e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} v$ and $w$ to $e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} w$, where $v$ and $w$ are the eigenvectors of $P$ with eigenvalues $1$ and $-1$ respectively.
