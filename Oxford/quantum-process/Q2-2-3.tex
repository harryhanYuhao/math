\subsubsection{Case 1,0} \label{section:case-1-0}

\paragraph{Algebraic Analysis}

The first part of our circuit is shown below.

\begin{equation}\label{solution-effect}
\tikzfig{q-2-2-case-1-0}
\end{equation}
Let us analyse what the circuit dose when the measurement outcomes are $1, 0$.

The input of the circuit is a state in $n$-qubit space.
Use our calculation in \eqref{q2-1-cal}, if $v$ is the eigenvector of $\vec{P}$ with eigenvalue $1$, and $w$ is the eigenvector with eigenvalue $-1$, the above circuit calculates as follows

\begin{equation}\label{q2-2-10-cal}
	\begin{split}
	v 
	&\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_1} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} |1\rangle \otimes v 
	\xrightarrow{\Pi^0 \vec{Q}_2} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|+\rangle \otimes v  \\
	w 
	&\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_1}  |0\rangle \otimes w 
	\xrightarrow{\Pi^0 \vec{Q}_2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|+\rangle \otimes w  \\
	\end{split}
\end{equation}
Using our notation in \eqref{q2-1}, we see that our desired operation, which is the left-hand side of \eqref{question:q2}, is 
$$
\sqrt{2} | 0 \rangle \otimes W 
$$
since $\tikzfig{X-state} = \sqrt{2} \ket{0}$.

Our calculation in \eqref{q2-1-cal} shows that, if $v$ is the eigenvector of $\vec{P}$ with eigenvalue $1$, and $w$ is the eigenvector with eigenvalue $-1$, the desired operation is
\begin{equation}
	\begin{split}
		v &\xrightarrow{\sqrt{2} | 0 \rangle \otimes W} \sqrt{2} |0\rangle \otimes v \\
		w &\xrightarrow{\sqrt{2} | 0 \rangle \otimes W} \sqrt{2} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} |0\rangle \otimes w
	\end{split}
\end{equation}

Comparing with \eqref{q2-2-10-cal}, we see we need to find a Clifford $C_{0,1}$ such that 
\begin{equation}
	\begin{split}
		C_{0, 1} |+\rangle \otimes v & = 2 e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} |0\rangle \otimes v \\
		C_{0, 1} |+\rangle \otimes w & = 2 e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} |0\rangle \otimes w \\
	\end{split}
\end{equation}

We decompose our Palui $\vec{P} = N^{\dagger} Z_1 N$ for some Clifford $N$, where $Z_1$ is the Pauli $Z$ acting on the first qubit. 
I claim that $C_{0,1} = H \otimes N^{\dagger} S_1 N$ works, where $S_1 = e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} |0 \rangle \langle 0| +  e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} |1 \rangle \langle 1|$.
The verification is straightforward, as $H |+\rangle = 2 |0\rangle$,
and $N^{\dagger}S_1N$ exactly sends $v$ to $e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} v$ and $w$ to $e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} w$ (upto a phase), where $v$ and $w$ are the eigenvectors of $P$ with eigenvalues $1$ and $-1$ respectively.
