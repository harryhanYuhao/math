\subsubsection{Case 1,1}

Using the same argument as above, let us calculate what does $\vec{Q}_1, \vec{Q}_2$ do with $j = k =1$.

\begin{equation}
	\begin{split}
	v 
	&\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_1} e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} |1\rangle \otimes v 
	\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_2} - e^{\frac{\pi}{4}i} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|-\rangle \otimes v  \\
	w 
	&\xrightarrow{\Pi^1 \vec{Q}_1}  |0\rangle \otimes w 
	\xrightarrow{\Pi^0 \vec{Q}_2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|-\rangle \otimes w  \\
	\end{split}
\end{equation}


Note, $HX|-\rangle = 2 |0 \rangle$, and using the same reasoning as above, we see
\begin{equation}
	\begin{split}
		(-P N^{\dagger} S_1 N) v &= - e^{-\frac{\pi}{4}} v \\
		(-P N^{\dagger} S_1 N) w &= e^{\frac{\pi}{4}} w \\	
	\end{split}
\end{equation}
Thus $C_{1,1} = (H X) \otimes (-P N^{\dagger} S_1 N)$ would work.
