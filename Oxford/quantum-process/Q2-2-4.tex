\subsubsection{Remark}\label{remark:alternating-pauli-clifford}

We have the following important lemma.

\begin{lemma}\label{lemma:measure-conjugate}
If a Pauli $P$ can be written as  $P = C P' C^\dagger$, where $C$ is a clifford and $P'$ is another Pauli, then 
\begin{equation}\label{eq:measure-conjugate}
	\Pi^{i} P = C \Pi^{i} P' C^\dagger
\end{equation}
where $\Pi^{i} P$ denote the ONB measurement in the eigenbasis of $P$ with outcome $i$.
\end{lemma}

\paragraph{Algebraic Analysis}

Our previous calculation shows that for outcome $i, j$, our post selected Clifford is 
\begin{enumerate}
	\item $C_{0, 0} = H \otimes \vec{I}$
	\item $C_{0, 1} = X H \otimes \vec{P} =  (X\otimes \vec{P}) (H \otimes \vec{I})$  
	\item $C_{1, 0} = H \otimes N^{\dagger} S_1 N $
	\item $C_{1, 1} = X H \otimes P N^{\dagger} S_1 N  =  ( X \otimes \vec{P}) (H \otimes N^\dagger S_1 N)$ 
\end{enumerate}

That is
Note that when the first measurement outcome is $0$, the post selected Clifford has the form $(H\otimes \vec{I}) D_{0,k}$ where $D_{0, k}$ is another Clifford depending on the second measurement outcome $k$.
Let us write our original circuit with outcoome $0, k$ as
$$
\Pi^0 \vec{Q_1} \Pi^k\vec{Q_2} C_{0, k}
$$
Using \eqref{eq:measure-conjugate} it is equivalent to
$$
\Pi^0 \vec{Q_1} (H \otimes \vec{I}) \Pi^k\vec{Q_2'} D_{0, k}
$$
where $\vec{Q_2'} = (H\otimes \vec{I})^\dagger \vec{Q_2} (H\otimes \vec{I})$.

Similarly, when the first measurement outcome is $1$, we can use the same argument to show
\begin{align*}
	\Pi^1 \vec{Q_1} \Pi^k\vec{Q_2} C_{1, k}
	&= \Pi^1 \vec{Q_1} (X H \otimes \vec{P}) \Pi^k\vec{Q_2'} D_{1, k} 
\end{align*}
Where $D_{1, k}$ is another Clifford depending on the second measurement outcome $k$, and $\vec{Q_2'} = (X H \otimes \vec{P})^\dagger \vec{Q_2} (X H \otimes \vec{P})$.

In either case, we have rewritten our original circuit as
$$
\Pi^i \vec{Q_1} \Pi^k\vec{Q_2} C_{i, k} = \Pi^i \vec{Q_1} E_i  \Pi^k\vec{Q_{2, i}'} D_{k}
$$
where Pauli measurements alternates Clifford gates and the subsequent Clifford gate and Pauli measurement are only dependent on previous measurement outcomes.
